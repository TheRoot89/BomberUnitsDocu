3 Player Management
4 =================
5 
6 The player management shall wrap all tasks related to tracking which players (local and remote) are
7 connected to the game and provide services to them.
8 
9 Requirements
10 ------------
11 
12 1. The player management state shall be persistent over the application runtime.
13 2. It shall keep track of all connected players, local and remote
14 3. It shall provide the logic behind adding/removing new players (local or remote) and limitation thereof
15  by a configured maximum amount of players permitted.
16 4. It shall interact with the UI to allow for customization of local players.
17 5. It shall be able to save/restore the host player settings upon application start.
18 
19 Architecture
20 ------------
21 
22 The architecture describes the SW components and allocation of responsibilities between them.
23 A player is a physical user's representation in the game, where that user may be part of the host's players
24 or a connected client's players.
25 
26 ### PlayerManager
27 
28 * The PlayerManager class follows the Singleton pattern and is thus accessible statically.
29  The script's gameObject shall be persistent across scenes (Req. 1).
30 * It contains lists of both player types (Req. 2).
31 * It shall check for whether more local and total players are allowed and provide an "Add Player" button
32  if so. The button shall be disabled once the maximum amount of players has been reached (Req. 3).
33 * The PlayerManager provides a way of checking whether a client is able to join with all his local players
34  (Req. 3).
35 * It shall react to networked PlayerAddedEvents:
36  * Provide a local UIPlayerSlot for displaying
37 * It interacts with the PlayerPrefs utility to save/restore the host's player (Req. 5).
38 
39 ### Player
40 
41 The Player class wraps all information on a participating player.
42 
43 * From Req. 2: A Player instance is marked local or remote.
44 * A Player has a reference to it's slot and triggers an update of the slot's representation when its data has
45  changed.
46 
47 Should allocated functionality differ regarding the above roles, a split into two subclassed to a common
48 'Player' base class should be considered.
49 
50 ### UIPlayerSlot
51 
52 Based on Req. 4:<br>
53 
54 * The UIPlayerSlot represents a UI component able to adequately represent the player's current settings, such
55  as name, color and InputScheme.
56 * A click on the slot's UI shall open up a customization dialog for local players.
57 
58 ### UIPlayerCustDialog
59 
60 This class shall be a subclass of a so-called 'UIDialog', which manages a blocking pop-up in-game or in
61 menus. For this Prefabs are used, not references to existing instances. Also after the dialog has ended, the
62 instance is destroyed, not only disabled. The script is therefore a component of the dialog.
63 
64 A specific dialog subclass handles the special content of the pop-up.
