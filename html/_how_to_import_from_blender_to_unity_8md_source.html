3 How to import from Blender 2.8 to Unity
4 ==============
5 
6 1. Save your object to somewhere in the scene.
7 2. Do the UV and the Lightmap. In the UV Editing workspace, you can have a lightmap auto-generated or put it
8  up manually. This may be necessary if the automatic creation of a lightmap through Unity doesn't proof
9  to be good. No lightmap is needed for dynamic objects anyway.
10 3. Apply all modifiers. (For many objects select and Object -> Convert to mesh)
11 4. Combine the objects (fewer in-game objects in Unity!) (use Ctrl+j)
12 5. File->Export->.FBX (-z forward, y up)
13  Tick the box for "Selected objects only", so you don't get the camera, light etc. too.
14  Save to the Unity Assets/Models folder.
15 6. Select in Unity and adjust the options. By default, "Generate Lightmap" can be used with a medium margin.
16 7. If your object shall be static, mark it as such and bake the lightmap and check for overlaps with your new
17  model added.
